Question 2: Performance on Inpainting Tasks.

The figure illustrates an inpainting experiment conducted on two distinct 512x512x3 images: one depicting bricks (top row) and the other, the renowned Lena image (bottom row). The first column displays the original ground truth images, while the second column shows these images with a continuous 10% mask applied, simulating a missing area. The third column presents the reconstruction achieved using our PuTT method. The top row highlights the method's proficiency in structured settings, as evidenced by the successful inpainting of the brick image. Conversely, the bottom row with the Lena image elucidates the challenges faced in inpainting extensive regions without sophisticated priors, relying solely on the inherent statistics of the image. Both reconstructions were obtained after 1024 iterations, using only samples from the unmasked regions of the respective images.
